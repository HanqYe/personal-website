Qiye Han

Economics · Renmin University of China · Exchange at UChicago

I am a third-year undergraduate in Economics at Renmin University of China, currently on a one-year exchange at the University of Chicago. My research interests center on how technological innovation shapes investment—from platform design and investor behavior to NLP-enabled measurement in financial research. I am particularly drawn to questions at the intersection of fintech, behavioral economics, and asset pricing.

I have research experience working with Professor Celine Yue Fei on ESG investment and platform nudges, and with Professor Ziteng Lei on representation learning and embeddings. I am ranked in the top 5% of my cohort and aspire to pursue a PhD in Finance or Economics.

Research Interests

Platform Design & Investor Behavior

How platform innovations and choice architecture reshape investment decisions. My work examines ESG investment on fintech platforms, uncovering substitution effects between offline green behavior and portfolio allocation. I aim to design and evaluate causal nudge experiments on financial platforms.

NLP & Computational Finance

Applying NLP, LLMs, and representation learning to financial data. I study how embeddings trained on asset characteristics can capture economic structure beyond traditional factor models, with implications for asset pricing and portfolio construction.

Behavioral & Experimental Economics

Designing randomized controlled trials and quasi-experimental studies (DiD, event studies) to identify causal effects of platform nudges on investor behavior and pro-environment actions. Interested in heterogeneity analysis using causal forests.

Economic Theory & Modeling

Building theoretical models that clarify mechanisms behind empirical findings. Trained in dynamic equilibrium analysis and intertemporal allocation at UChicago, with interest in how fintech reshapes market structure and competition.
